Projects

Graduate Student Researcher

University of California, Riverside | Advisor: Associate Professor Chen Li

  • Machine Learning-Based Force Constant Extraction from INS Measurements of Polycrystalline Materials (first) (manuscript in preparation)
    • Tags: Machine Learnig, Computer Vision, Data Analysis, Neutron scatteirng, Condensed Matter Physics
    • Developed and implemented variational autoencoder models to transform intricate inelastic neutron scattering (INS) spectra into a condensed and informative latent representation, serving as a foundation for subsequent tasks.
    • Utilized a neural network regressor to retrieve force constants from the learned latent representation.
    • Conducted exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, and model selection to optimize model performance.
    • Evaluated the optimal model on experimental spectra obtained at different temperatures and demonstrated that our method exhibits exceptional consistency and reliability compared to traditional optimization methods.
  • Intrinsically low thermal conductivity and lattice dynamics in single crystals of garnet-type solid electrolyte (co-first) (manuscript in preparation)
    • Tags: Li-ion batteries with solid-state electrolytes (SSEs), Data Analysis, Neutron scatteirng, Condensed Matter Physics
    • Designed and performed INS measurements to study the phonon dynamics and thermal transport properties of solid electrolyte Li6.5La3Zr1.5Ta0.5O12(LLZTO).
    • Analyzed and visualized INS data to identify the acoustic phonon modes of LLZTO.
    • Applied quasi-elastic scattering analysis to investigate the diffusion of lithium ions in the material.
